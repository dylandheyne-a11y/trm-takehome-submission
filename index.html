🚀 Step 1: "High growth, fast-paced startups"

Respond to the questions below:

QuestionYour response
Write your definition of a "High growth, fast-paced startup" A high-growth, fast-paced startup is a company where the business is scaling faster than the operating system around it. The work usually has three signals: strong talent density, urgent customer or market pull, and a culture where people are expected to make clear tradeoffs without perfect information.
How did you come up with this definition? I used Paraform's Talent Density Index as the main model because it looks at the quality and concentration of talent, not just brand name or funding. I then cross-checked that against the actual profiles we reviewed so I did not treat "well-known company" as the same thing as "right environment." Paraform also uses a supply/demand model, which is an excellent market signal. To consider alternatives, I sourced counter-points here: trm-company-guide-paraform.html
Why would this be a pre-requisite for most positions at TRM? TRM's posting is explicit: priorities change quickly, pace is high, ambiguity is normal, and engineers are expected to anchor projects. Someone from a slower, highly-defined environment may still be strong, but the search should bias toward people who have already operated with urgency, ownership, and incomplete information.

2. Glean - enterprise search, permissions, connectors, and product/platform engineering.

Share the AI Agent Here

Primary demo: TRM Candidate Review Workstation

This is the lightweight agent/workflow I would show live: paste a LinkedIn/profile summary, score it against the rubric, generate false-positive flags, create X-ray searches, log the result, and export the evidence to CSV.

What makes it more than a prompt

  • Market mapper: turns the JD into company targets, team lanes, and Boolean blocks.
  • Profile evaluator: reviews pasted LinkedIn evidence against the role rubric and logs structured output.
  • Calibration loop: compares first-pass scores against Claude/human review and updates false-positive rules.
  • Red-team layer: challenges overclaims, weak company logic, and candidates who look strong only because of brand or keywords.
  • Submission layer: turns the work into HM-ready Slack messages, outreach, and panel-ready explanation.

The calibration loop is the important part. The system initially over-scored profiles when company brand or keywords looked good but the actual function was wrong. I adjusted the workflow after misses like frontend-primary profiles, ML infra/FDE profiles, API consumers, and company-prior false positives.

AI Usage Note

I used AI as a workflow layer: research assistant, market mapper, profile reviewer, calibration partner, skeptic, and writing editor.

Where AI materially improved the work

  • Translated the JD into search criteria and evaluation dimensions.
  • Built a target-company and team guide using Paraform as the foundation.
  • Generated and refined Boolean blocks for company/team sourcing.
  • Built a local candidate review workstation to score pasted profile evidence and log analysis.
  • Compared tool output against Claude/human calibration and corrected false positives.
  • Turned calibration notes into reusable rules for future searches.

Where calibration changed the system

  • Company prior was too loud. Vanta, Glean, Stripe, Ramp, and Linear are useful sources, but company alone cannot carry a candidate.
  • Keywords were not enough. The tool initially overvalued words like API, platform, backend, built, and led without checking what was actually built.
  • False positives needed caps. Frontend-primary, ML infra/FDE, GRC/analyst, API-consumer, and client-implementation profiles now get flagged instead of pushed forward.
  • Fraud/risk/security product work was underweighted. Stripe Fraud, Coinbase risk, and security API work map strongly to TRM even without crypto experience.
  • Thin LinkedIn evidence needed a softer path. Some candidates should become validate/probe, not automatic pass or automatic reject.

Where I overrode AI

  • Company name alone was not enough to advance a profile.
  • Keyword matches were not enough when the functional archetype was wrong.
  • Thin LinkedIn evidence became a validation item, not a reason to invent detail.
  • Fraud/risk/security product work was treated as highly relevant to TRM, even when the first-pass tool underweighted it.

How I would explain this live

I would show the company guide, then the candidate workstation, then the calibration log. The point is not that AI produced the final answer. The point is that AI helped me move faster, see more options, catch misses, and make the judgment process inspectable.
